Year: 1888
Art movement: Impressionism
Medium: Oil on canvas
Dimensions: 43.2 x 53.3 cm

Description: In the summer of 1888, Sargent and his family moved to Calcot Mill in Berkshire. This autumnal scene was painted during a boat trip with Flora Priestley, the first owner of the picture. The painting is characterized by swift execution, fresh composition, vibrant brushwork, an uncluttered palette, and a rain-washed sky. An amusing anecdote attached to the work involves Sargent's distraction by Priestley, resulting in a heavily pigmented canvas. On the journey back, the canvas was scratched by willows, adding to its character. Despite these imperfections, the painting is notable for its lively execution and charming backstory. Charles Merritt Mount and others have appreciated these elements in Sargent's work.
