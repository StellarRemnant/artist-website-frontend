Year: 1912
Art movement: Realism
Medium: Charcoal
Dimensions: 62.1 x 47.9 cm

Description: The work was commissioned by Mrs Dorothy Walker Heneage, together with a portrait of herself, as the pair were close friends. In a letter dating from March 1912 Sargent notes that he was pleased that Dorothy had a nice recollection of sitting for her portrait, but he couldn’t believe that Earl Fitzwilliam would wish to be drawn again or submit to the operation ‘without anaesthetics’ (S. Walker-Heneage, op. cit., p. 183).
