Year: 1890

Medium: Oil on canvas
Dimensions: 218.76 × 122.56

Description: In December 1889, Sargent returned to the United States and quickly received numerous portrait requests from New York and Boston society. In June 1890, he painted Mrs. Edward Livingston Davis and her son in Worcester, Massachusetts. The portrait, created in the Davises’ stable, focuses on their figures, highlighting their character and relationship. Sargent contrasts Mrs. Davis' upper-class elegance with her warmth as a mother. The interaction between mother and son is depicted through their tender grasp of hands. Sargent's use of a dark palette and strong lighting creates a bright, colorful effect, showcasing his fluid brushwork and manipulation of dramatic lighting. The painting is both a psychological exploration and a technical masterpiece, reflecting Spanish baroque influences.
